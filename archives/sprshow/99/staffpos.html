Spring Show 1999: Jesus Christ Superstar

Staff Application: Staff Positions and Descriptions


So you want to apply to be on the JCS staff? Well, here's what you need to do.
  1. Take a look at the staff positions and descriptions below.
  2. Fill out and submit the on-line application by Wednesday, November 23, 1998.
  3. You will be contacted to schedule an interview, soon after.

Designers and directors should be able to attend a one-night design retreat before the end of Fall Quarter. People working on the show should plan on staying on campus over Spring Break to assist with Build.

Alrighty? If you have any questions or need more information, contact producer Molly McKew at cdrake@leland.

Here is a list of staff positions and a general description of their duties. Of course descriptions are flexible and will change once I actually have a staff. Be sure to bring ideas that you have for your own job definition to your interview (I feel like the teacher who just gave away the essay question).

Production Staff

Assistant Producers - helping me, the producer, with whatever I need help with. These include:

  • Publicity Manager - in charge of the creation of production posters and flyers, press releases, teaser creation and coordination and contacts with the media.
  • Graphics Artist - in charge of working with the Publicity and Program Managers to turn ideas into reality. Photoshop and other program knowledge useful.
  • Business Manager - in charge of monitoring the production budget with the producer and the various designers, organizing general production accounting (receipts and reimbursements) and soliciting corporate sponsorship for the show.
  • Program Manager - in charge of the preparation of the program and the selling of ads for the program. Also works with the Business Manager on obtaining a corporate sponsor.
  • House Manager - in charge of preparing Mem Aud for the production, oversees ushers and potentially lobby food vendors.
  • Ticket Manager - in charge of block-ticket sales, ticket creation, and general box office management.
  • Social Manager - in charge of the creation of fun. Makes sure that a rockin' retreat and cast party happens, that spring break is fun, that everyone on the cast and crew is happy and working well together, and that a party is constantly going on that everyone is invited to. Knowledge of j-ello crucial.
  • Photographer - to chronicle the production in still life.
  • Assistant positions available for each manager.

Artistic Staff

  • Director - in charge of entire artistic production and providing theoverall artistic vision.
  • Music Director - in charge of coordinating vocal aspects, working with the orchestra and the Vocal Director.
  • Vocal Director - in charge of make the singers sound wonderful.
  • Choreographer - in charge of coordinating dance aspects.
  • Fight Choreographer - in charge of staging scuffles.
  • Dramaturg - in charge of doing research to assist the actors and designers in understanding the show
  • Technical Director - in charge of organizing all things technical and making sure the design staff works together towards the director's goal.
  • Set Designer - in charge of the creation of the set and its implementation, especially during Build Week.
  • Costume Designer - in charge of the creation and organization of the costumes, as well as hair and makeup designs.
  • Light Designer - in charge of the creation of a lighting design.
  • Sound Designer - in charge of the creation of a sound design.
  • Props Person - in charge of the creation and organization of the props.
  • Stage Manager - in charge of coordinating the rehearsals, calling the show, and assisting the director with organizational aspects of the show -- among other things.
  • Run Crew Chief - in charge of the run crew for the run of the show.
  • Crew (any scenic painters or people who wish to be master electricians or shop foremen out there) and assistant positions available.

On to the application!
