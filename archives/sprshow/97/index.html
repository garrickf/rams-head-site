In Brief...
Set in 1940's Hollywood, the 1989 Tony-award winning musical "City of Angels" simultaneously tells the stories of a struggling screen writer named Stine and his creation named Stone. The audience not only watches Stine maneuver his way through Hollywood, but is able to see his work, through an on-stage mini-private eye movie starring Stone, a Raymond Chandler-esque gumshoe detective. The show is color coded, with the real life passages staged in vivid colors and the movie world in black and white, and features a jazzy score.
