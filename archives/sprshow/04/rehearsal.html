Staff Job Descriptions

Director
The Director is the artistic manager of the production.  He/she is responsible for coordinating the work of the cast, designers, and crew to uphold the vision of the show.  The Director will assist in the hiring of some of the staff, and is responsible for the vision and integrity of the production.  The Director will be present for all auditions and design meetings and will direct the actors.  The Director, with the Vocal Director and the Choreographer, will select the cast.  The Director will meet early and often with the Designers.

Choreographer
 The Choreographer will design the dance and movement elements of the show.  The blocking of the musical will largely be the choreographer’s responsibility.  For “Kiss Me Kate,” the Choreographer will, along with the Vocal Director and the Director, select the cast members.  The choreographer will also be instrumental in creating the vision of the show along with the Director.

Technical Director
 The Technical Director is the administrative head of the technical aspects of the show.  The TD examines the technical feasibility of the designs for the show, and oversees implementation of those concepts.  The TD supervises the progress of designs, and arranges for the hiring of crew to implement the designs.  The TD coordinates budgetary concerns with the Producer and helps maintain communication between designers, crew and the rest of the staff.

Stage Manager (ass. Stage manager- prop master)
 The Stage Manager is the liaison between the actors and all things technical.  He/she is in charge of calling the show from back stage.  The Stage Manager becomes the leadership and command figure for the first tech rehearsal.  He/she will facilitate communication between staff and actors.  The Stage Manager will be in contact with the Production Manager to relay information to the business staff.  The Stage Manager will take down all information from the designers regarding the functions of the technical cues.  The Stage Manager alerts the cast of notes and schedules.  The Stage Manager organizes and runs auditions.

Vocal Director
 The Vocal Director will oversee the learning and singing of all songs in “Kiss Me Kate.”  He/she must lead all vocal rehearsals.  The Vocal Director, with the Musical Director, is responsible for upholding the musical integrity of the show.  The Vocal Director will cast the show with the Director and the Choreographer.

Musical Director
 The Musical Director is responsible for recruiting and running the orchestra.  He/she will oversee the learning of the entire score of “Kiss Me Kate.”   The Musical Director will conduct the orchestra for all rehearsals and performances involving the orchestra.  The Musical Director, with the Vocal Director, is responsible for the musical integrity of the show.
