She wants to thank her family and Michael for their love and support, the friends who wormed their way into my schedule, Roth for keeping things exciting, the SM family past and present, and most importantly, the cast and staff who made every minute of this process a reminder of why she chose to produce in the first place.

Andrew M. Reid (Set Designer) is honored and somewhat surprised to be designing his sixth Ram's Head Spring Musical. It has been a great experience working with this great cast, staff, and crew on Cabaret and he hopes you enjoy the show as much as he enjoyed working on it. Andrew previously set designed West Side Story, 2003; sound designed Company, 2005; and lighting designed Kiss Me Kate, 2004; The Mystery of Edwin Drood, 2002; A Chorus Line , 2001; and Gaieties 2002, 2003, & 2004 for Ram's Head. For the Stanford Drama Department, he has lighting designed Mother Courage, 2005; Maria, 2004; Measure for Measure, 2002; and Entertaining Mr. Sloan, 2002 and has worked as an assistant lighting designer at Broadway by the Bay in San Mateo.

Storm Staley (Lighting Designer) has been a fixture in the Ram's Head organization for a few years now, to the point that people are wondering when he'll finally leave. Amazingly, however, this is the first spring musical for which he has done lighting design, having been too busy in the past, either with sound design or his long-pursued goal of not letting theatre take over his life. (He has failed outstandingly on that last, given the circumstances under which you are reading this). He wishes to thank his fabulous programmer, Chelsea Vargas, his assistant lighting designer, Sarah Young, and his two excellent spotlight operators. His lighting design for Cabaret is inspired by the thought that 1930s Berlin nightclubs could have been so much better had they been jazzed up with some 1980s lighting technology. (Stanford doesn't have up-to-date lighting technology either). Enjoy the show.
