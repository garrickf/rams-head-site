The Producer
 Back to table of contents
The producer is basically in charge of the show.
The producer is responsible for hiring the staff, creating and maintaining the budget and coordinating with the Ram's Head Board of directors. The producer works for Ram's head and for the show. Everyone else works for him/her.
 

Ram's Head policy on the producer
D.Individual Show Producers
             1.General The Producer is responsible for all aspects of the production and is answerable to the Ram's head Board
               of Directors and Executive Producer.
             2.Eligibility The Producer must be a registered student for some portion of his/her term of office. It is recommended
               that the Producer have some theatrical experience, preferably with the production of some previous Ram's Head
               show.
             3.Selection The Producer shall be selected, through an interview process, by a committee consisting of one
               Board-appointed Board member, the past Producer, the Executive Producer, and the new Executive Producer, if
               chosen, and with the consent of the Board of Directors, by the following deadlines:
                    The Big Game Gaieties: February 1.
                    The Original Winter One Acts: six weeks after the final performance.
                    Spring Show: May 15.
             4.Duties and Responsibilities
                    The Producer is responsible for the selection of his/her staff and the well-being of the production.
                    The Producer is responsible for the finances of the production and insuring the accuracy of the Financial
                    Manager's records.
                    The Producer is responsible for developing a realistic budget with the help of the Executive Producer,
                    Financial Manager and past Producers, and seeing that the production staff operates within that budget.
                    The Producer's term of office commences upon selection and terminates after all aspects of that production
                    have been completed as determined by the Executive Producer.
                    The Producer must report regularly on the progress of the production to both the Board and to the
                    Executive Producer.
